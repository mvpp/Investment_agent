Below are the most relevant financial & operating metrics to industries (short rationale + a few calculation / benchmark notes). Use this as a practical checklist when you compare companies inside an industry or build screening rules.

## Cross-industry core metrics (always check these)

- Revenue / Revenue growth — top-line momentum.
- Gross margin / EBITDA margin / Net margin — unit economics and operating leverage.
- Free cash flow (FCF) / FCF margin — cash generation after capex.
- ROIC / ROCE — how well capital is deployed (especially important where capital is durable).
- Debt metrics: Net debt / EBITDA, Interest coverage.
- Valuation multiples: P/E (if profitable), EV/EBITDA, EV/Revenue, P/S, P/B (financials/asset heavy).
- Cash runway / burn (for unprofitable growth companies).

## Industry-by-industry cheat sheet
1. Software / SaaS

Key metrics: `ARR / YoY ARR growth, Net Revenue Retention (NRR), Gross margin, Rule of 40 (growth % + margin %), CAC, CAC payback, LTV:CAC, Gross churn & logo churn, FCF margin (once mature), EV/Revenue or P/S`.

  Why: Revenue is recurring; growth + margin tradeoff matters (Rule of 40); retention and unit economics drive long-term value.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - ARR / Revenue growth > 25%. (high growth expected)
  > - Net Revenue Retention (NRR) > 100%. (expansion > churn)
  > - Rule of 40 (growth % + EBITDA% or FCF%) > 0.
  > - EV/Revenue < 10x for growth stage (looser for breakout names).

2. Internet / Marketplaces / Platforms

Key metrics: `GMV (gross merchandise volume) growth, Take-rate, Contribution margin per transaction, ARPU / Revenue per MAU or DAU, CAC, LTV, NRR (if subscription parts), EV/GMV or EV/Revenue, engagement metrics`.

  Why: Platform monetization (take-rate) and network effects drive scalability — user engagement and unit economics matter more than headline revenue early on.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - GMV or Revenue growth > 30% (or user growth >30%).
  > - Take-rate improvement or contribution margin per transaction > 15%.
  > - CAC payback < 18 months and repeat usage / retention rising.
  > - EV/Revenue or EV/GMV in line with peers (no extreme premium).

3. Hardware & Consumer Electronics

Key metrics: `Gross margin, Inventory turnover / days, Return rates/warranty costs, R&D/sales, Capex/sales, EV/EBITDA, Free cash flow`.

  Why: Capital & inventory intensive; margins and supply chain/returns control profitability.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - YoY revenue growth ≥ 5% (or stable).
  > - Gross margin > 20% and inventory turns > 4x.
  > - Net debt / EBITDA < 3x and FCF positive.

4. Semiconductors

Key metrics: `Book-to-bill, ASP trends, Fab utilization, Gross margin, Capex intensity (capex/ sales), Inventory days, ROIC, EV/EBITDA`.

  Why: Cyclical demand + heavy capex; utilization and pricing determine profitability.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - Book-to-bill ≥ 1.0 or improving.
  > - Gross margin > 40% (or improving) and fab utilization rising.
  > - Capex / Sales < 20% (ID high-capex risk) and Net debt / EBITDA < 3–4x.

5. Infrastructure / Utilities / Regulated Assets (incl. some Telecom)

Key metrics: `ROIC / regulated return on RAB, EBITDA margin, FCF yield, Capex / Depreciation, Net debt / EBITDA, Interest coverage, Dividend payout ratio`.

  Why: High capital intensity and predictable cashflows — returns on invested capital and leverage management are central (valuation often via FCF yield or regulated ROE).
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - ROIC (or regulated return) > 6–8% (above cost of capital).
  > - FCF yield > 5% (or dividend yield > industry median).
  > - Net debt / EBITDA < 4x and interest coverage > 3–4x.

6. Energy & Oil & Gas (Upstream / Midstream / Downstream)

Key metrics: `Production volumes, Realized price per boe, Lifting cost / cash cost per boe, EBITDA per boe, Reserve life index, Net debt / EBITDA, FCF, EV / proved reserves (2P), Capex`.

  Why: Commodity price exposure and capex cycles — per-unit economics and balance sheet strength are decisive.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - Production growth or stable production; realized price sensitivity hedged.
  > - Lifting / cash cost per boe in bottom 50% of peer group.
  > - Net debt / EBITDA < 2.5–3x and EV / 2P reserves reasonable vs peers.

7. Real Estate / REITs

Key metrics: `FFO / AFFO per share, NOI (net operating income), Same-store NOI growth, Occupancy rate, Lease maturity profile, LTV (loan-to-value), Cap rate, Dividend yield`.

  Why: Earnings definition differs (use FFO/AFFO rather than GAAP EPS); asset values and cash yield drive returns.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - FFO / AFFO growth > 3% and occupancy > 90% (or sector norm).
  > - LTV (loan-to-value) < 55% and same-store NOI growth ≥ 2%.
  > - Dividend yield within peer range and cap rate vs market favorable.

8. Banks

Key metrics: `Net interest margin (NIM), Loan growth, Non-performing loans / NPL ratio, Provision coverage, CET1 / regulatory capital ratios, Efficiency ratio (cost/income), ROE / ROTCE, P/B`.

  Why: Intermediation business — spread, asset quality and capital adequacy are central.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - NIM stable or improving and loan growth ≥ 1–5% (depending on cycle).
  > - NPL ratio < 2% (or provision coverage healthy).
  > - CET1 ratio > 10–12% and P/B not excessively high (> peer median).

9. Insurance

Key metrics: `Combined ratio (loss + expense), Loss ratio, Expense ratio, Reserve adequacy, Investment yield, Premium growth, Solvency / RBC ratios, ROE`.

  Why: Underwriting profitability + investment returns determine economics.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - Combined ratio < 100% (underwriting profitable) or improving.
  > - Reserve leverage / solvency metrics (company-specific) above regulatory minima.
  > - Investment yield + underwriting margin produce ROE > industry median.

10. Pharmaceuticals

Key metrics: `R&D/sales, Gross margin, EBITDA margin, Revenue concentration by product, Patent expiry timelines (patent cliff), Pipeline value (probabilistic NPV), EV/Revenue or EV/EBITDA for mature players`.

  Why: Blockbuster concentration and pipeline risk; R&D productivity and patent protection are crucial.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - Revenue diversification: no single product > 25–30% of revenue (or pipeline offsets risk).
  > - R&D / Sales in range for peers (not extreme), and gross margin > 60%.
  > - Pipeline milestones visibility / patent life: positive near-term catalysts.

11. Biotech (clinical stage)

Key metrics: `Cash runway / burn rate, Milestone and trial success probabilities, R&D spend, Dilution risk (expected financing rounds), Value per pipeline asset, Partnerships / licensing revenues`.

  Why: Often pre-revenue — balance sheet and binary event risk dominate valuation.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - Cash runway ≥ 12–18 months (or financing plan visible).
  > - Binary event cadence: upcoming catalysts priced in; burn rate reasonable.
  > - Partnership/license interest or non-dilutive funding reduces financing risk.

12. Medical Devices

Key metrics: `Gross margin, Consumables / recurring revenue %, Reimbursement exposure, R&D/sales, ASP trends, EV/Revenue for growth names, ROIC`.

  Why: Mix of hardware + consumables; recurring consumables increase predictability.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - Recurring revenue / consumables > 25% of sales (stickiness).
  > - Gross margin > 50% and R&D / Sales stable with positive product cycle.
  > - EV/Revenue reasonable vs growth profile; ROIC > peers once mature.

13. Retail (Brick & Mortar)

Key metrics: `Same-store sales (SSS) growth, Sales per sq ft (or AUV), Gross margin %, Inventory turnover, Store count growth, Operating margin, EBITDA margin`.

  Why: Footfall and inventory control drive profitability; store economics and comps matter most.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - Same-store sales (SSS) YoY ≥ 0–3% (or beating sector comps).
  > - Inventory turnover > 6x (or improving) and gross margin stable.
  > - Store-level EBITDA margin positive; net debt / EBITDA < 3x.

14. E-commerce / DTC

Key metrics: `GMV/revenue growth, Contribution margin per order, CAC, LTV, Return rate, Fulfillment cost per order, Repeat purchase rate, Gross margin, EBITDA margin`.

  Why: Customer economics and fulfillment efficiency determine sustainable unit economics.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - Revenue growth > 20% and repeat purchase rate > 30%.
  > - Contribution margin per order (after fulfillment & marketing) > 10%.
  > - CAC payback < 12 months or path to profitability visible.

15. Consumer Staples

Key metrics: `Stable revenue growth, Gross margin, EBITDA margin, Free cash flow yield, Inventory turnover, ROIC, Dividend yield, Brand strength metrics`.

  Why: Defensive, brand driven — cash generation and margins more important than high growth metrics.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - Stable revenue growth > 2% and gross margin > 30%.
  > - FCF yield > 4% and dividend yield in line with peers.
  > - ROIC > industry median and low leverage (Net debt/EBITDA < 2.5x).

16. Industrials / Capital Goods

Key metrics: `Order backlog / book-to-bill, EBIT margin, ROIC, Capex/sales, Working capital days, Revenue per employee, Backlog quality`.

  Why: Demand cycles and backlog visibility affect future revenue; capital deployment matters.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - Order backlog growth / book-to-bill ≥ 1.0.
  > - EBIT margin > 6% (or improving) and ROIC > 8%.
  > - Working capital days improving and Net debt / EBITDA < 3x.

17. Transportation / Airlines

Key metrics: `Load factor, RPK/ASK (or passengers per available seat), Yield (revenue per passenger mile), CASM (cost per ASM), Fuel cost exposure, Debt / EBITDAR, Liquidity`.

  Why: High operating leverage and cyclical demand — unit revenue and cost per unit are primary drivers.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - Load factor near or above industry average and yield trending up.
  > - CASM ex-fuel declining or stable; unit revenue recovery visible.
  > - Liquidity buffer ≥ 6–12 months of operating cash needs; Net debt / EBITDAR < 4–5x.

18. Materials / Mining

Key metrics: `Production volumes, Cash cost per tonne/oz, Realized commodity price, EBITDA per unit, Reserve life, Stripping ratio, Net debt / EBITDA`.

  Why: Commoditized, capital-intensive — margins are driven by realized commodity prices vs unit costs.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - Production growth or stable; cash cost per tonne/oz in lower half of peers.
  > - Reserve life healthy (company-specific) and stripping ratio acceptable.
  > - Net debt / EBITDA < 2.5–3x and EBITDA per unit robust vs peers.

19. Restaurants / Franchised Food

Key metrics: `Same-store sales, AUV (average unit volume), Unit economics (payback period), Food cost %, Labor cost %, Store margins, Store openings, Franchisee vs corporate mix`.

  Why: Unit economics (per store) and comparable sales are primary value levers.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - Same-store sales growth ≥ 0–3% (or positive vs comps).
  > - Unit-level payback period < 3–4 years for new openings.
  > - Store-level margins positive and franchisee economics intact (if franchised model).

20. Telecom (non-regulated parts)

Key metrics: `ARPU, Churn, Net adds, EBITDA margin, Capex intensity, Spectrum amortization, EBITDA per subscriber, Net debt / EBITDA`.

  Why: Subscriber metrics + ARPU indicate revenue health; capex and leverage are large constraints.
  > Simple Screening rules:
  > - ARPU growing or stable and churn < 1.5–2% monthly (or sector norm).
  > - EBITDA margin > 30% and Capex / Sales < 20–25% (manageable).
  > - Net debt / EBITDA < 3–4x and interest coverage > 3x.

## How to choose the right metrics for analysis

- Ask the economic question — Is value driven by recurring revenue (SaaS), per-unit commodity price (mining), asset base & regulated returns (utilities), or intermediation spread (banks)?

- Capital intensity — if capex is large and durable, prioritize ROIC / Capex/Sales / FCF.

- Recurring vs transactional — for recurring businesses, retention (NRR) and LTV matter; for transaction businesses, take-rate and contribution margin matter.

- Cyclicality / commodity exposure — focus on per-unit cash cost and leverage.

- Stage of company — early growth → ARR growth, burn & runway; mature → margins, ROIC, dividend yield, FCF.

## Pitfalls / watch-outs

- Comparing across industries: EV/Revenue or P/S is meaningful for SaaS but misleading for banks or REITs.

- Accounting differences: FFO/AFFO for REITs; combined ratio for insurers; provisioning for banks changes earnings quality.

- One-off items & cyclical noise: Normalize for cyclical commodities, seasonal inventory swings, or tax/asset sales.

- Unit metrics matter: Don’t ignore operating KPIs (CAC, churn, load factor) even if financials look OK.

- **It only makes sense to judge the valuation of a stock by examining its future free cash flow. Some stocks might seem over-valued, but if you analyze its business carefully, you might found it actually still cheap to buy. Say a stock has a EV/S of 40. Intimidating, right? but if you noticed that the EBITDA margin for its incremental income is at 75% or reaching 85% in the near future, and the conversion rate from EBITDA to FCF is 95%. It's quite a value investment as long as it maintains a 30% CAGR or more. Ultimately, business is about forward looking cash flow and its stability, consistency and certainty.**
